My story

I started out as a Java developer and Salesforce specialist, but I kept gravitating toward the data underneath every application — how it moves, how it's trusted, and how it scales. That pull led me into the big-data world, and I haven't looked back since. Today I work at the intersection of massive-scale infrastructure and the small, careful decisions that keep it reliable.

What I love most about data engineering is that it rewards both sides of my brain: the systems-thinker who enjoys architecting a 120-petabyte platform, and the tinkerer who'll happily spend an afternoon shaving minutes off a Spark job. I'm at my best when I'm turning something fragile and slow into something dependable and fast.

Beyond work

When I step away from clusters and pipelines, you'll usually find me with music playing — it's my reset button — or gathered around a good board game, where I get to indulge the same love of strategy and pattern-spotting that data work rewards. There's something about a well-designed game that scratches exactly the same itch as a well-tuned pipeline.

I also put real energy into volunteering — both technical and creative. Whether it's lending engineering skills to a cause or contributing on the creative side of an initiative, I care about using what I know to make a tangible difference for the communities and causes I believe in.
